Why Prompts Are Not Policy

Natural-language instructions to LLMs are non-deterministic, hard to test, and difficult to defend in regulated settings. Compliance requires certainty.

Core Principles

Policy-as-Code treats compliance like infrastructure ‚Äî versioned, testable, deployable, and rollback-ready.

1

Code, Not Text

Policies are written as executable logic, not ambiguous prose. Code is explicit ‚Äî it computes one result for a given input.

2

Version Controlled

Every policy change is a commit. Full history of who changed what, why, and when ‚Äî with the ability to revert quickly.

3

Test-Driven

Every rule has unit and regression tests covering normal cases, edge cases, and exception paths.

4

Continuously Deployed

Policy changes follow a release lifecycle: develop ‚Üí test ‚Üí stage ‚Üí promote to production.
