1. Introduction

1.1 Overview

Axiosky, Inc. ("we," "us," "our," or the "Company") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, share, and protect information when you:

  • Visit our website at https://axiosky.com (the "Site")
  • Use our governance control plane services, including the Governor, Policy Engine, and Orchestrator (the "Services")
  • Communicate with us via email, forms, or other channels

This Privacy Policy applies to information collected through the Site and Services. It does not apply to information collected by third parties or through third-party websites linked from our Site.

1.2 Scope and Applicability

For website visitors: This Privacy Policy governs personal information collected through our public-facing website, contact forms, newsletter signups, and downloads.

For customers and users: This Privacy Policy governs personal information collected in connection with your use of the Services. Additional terms regarding Customer Data (data you process using the Services) are covered in our Data Processing Agreement (DPA) and Terms of Service.

Geographic applicability: This Privacy Policy is designed to comply with:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for individuals in the European Economic Area (EEA), UK, and Switzerland
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) for California residents
  • Other applicable privacy laws where we operate

1.3 Controller and Contact Information

Data Controller: Axiosky Inc.
Email: privacy@axiosky.com
Data Protection Officer (EU/UK): dpo@axiosky.com

If you are in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland and have privacy concerns, you may contact our DPO directly.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

Personal Information means information that identifies, relates to, or could reasonably be linked to an individual. We collect the following categories of Personal Information:

2.1.1 Identity and Contact Information

  • Full name
  • Job title
  • Work email address
  • Company/organization name
  • Phone number (if provided)
  • Mailing address (if provided)

Collected from: Contact forms, demo requests, account registration, support inquiries

2.1.2 Account and Authentication Information

  • Username and password (hashed and encrypted)
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) credentials
  • SSO identity provider information (e.g., Okta, Azure AD user ID)
  • API keys and access tokens (encrypted)

Collected from: Account creation, SSO integration, API access setup

2.1.3 Professional and Organizational Information

  • Industry/sector
  • Organization size
  • Country and region
  • Deployment model preferences (cloud, on-premises, air-gapped)
  • Use case description (e.g., government procurement automation)

Collected from: Demo requests, pilot applications, sales conversations

2.1.4 Usage and Interaction Data

  • IP addresses
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Device identifiers
  • Pages visited and features used
  • Timestamps and session duration
  • Referral source (how you found our Site)

Collected from: Website analytics, application logs, service telemetry

2.1.5 Communication Data

  • Content of emails, support tickets, and chat messages
  • Feedback and survey responses
  • Marketing preferences (e.g., newsletter opt-ins)

Collected from: Email correspondence, support interactions, feedback forms

2.2 Customer Data Separate from Personal Information

Customer Data is data that you, as a customer, upload, process, or transmit through the Services. This includes:

  • Policy code and configurations you write
  • Agent workflow definitions
  • Audit logs generated by the Governor
  • Decision records and governance outputs

We are a data processor for Customer Data. You (the customer) are the data controller and determine what data is processed. Our obligations regarding Customer Data are governed by our Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

Important: We do not use Customer Data to train AI models or for purposes unrelated to providing the Services to you.

2.3 Information We Do NOT Collect

We do not knowingly collect:

  • Personal information from children under 16 (or under 13 in the U.S.)
  • Sensitive personal information (e.g., health data, biometric data, financial account details) unless you choose to include it in Customer Data, in which case you are responsible for ensuring lawful processing
  • Precise geolocation data (we collect approximate location from IP addresses for regional service delivery)

3. How We Collect Information

3.1 Information You Provide Directly

We collect information you provide when you:

  • Fill out contact forms, demo requests, or pilot applications
  • Create an account or configure Services
  • Subscribe to newsletters or marketing communications
  • Participate in surveys or provide feedback
  • Contact our support team

3.2 Information Collected Automatically

We automatically collect information when you interact with our Site or Services using cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies.

3.2.1 Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to:

  • Maintain session state and user preferences
  • Analyze Site usage and performance via Google Analytics or similar tools
  • Deliver targeted marketing with your consent where required

Types of cookies we use:

  • Essential cookies: Required for Site and Services functionality (e.g., session authentication). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Track usage patterns to improve our Site and Services (e.g., Google Analytics). You may opt out via browser settings or tools like Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on.
  • Marketing cookies: Used to deliver relevant ads and measure campaign effectiveness where consent is required. You may opt out via cookie consent banners or browser settings.

Managing cookies: You can control cookies through your browser settings or our cookie consent banner where applicable. Note that disabling essential cookies may impair Site functionality.

3.2.2 Log Files and Analytics

We collect log data, including:

  • IP addresses (anonymized where possible)
  • Request timestamps and URLs
  • HTTP status codes and error messages
  • User agent strings (browser/device info)

Purpose: Security monitoring, troubleshooting, and service improvement

3.2.3 Service Telemetry

For customers using the Services, we collect operational metrics:

  • Governor decision counts and latency
  • Orchestrator workflow execution metrics
  • Policy deployment events
  • Error rates and performance data

Purpose: Service reliability, capacity planning, and support. Where possible, telemetry is aggregated and anonymized.

3.3 Information from Third Parties

3.3.1 Identity Providers (SSO)

When you authenticate via SSO (e.g., Okta, Azure AD), we receive:

  • User ID and email address
  • Group/role memberships for access control

We do not receive passwords; authentication is handled by your IdP.

3.3.2 Marketing and Sales Tools

We use third-party tools (e.g., HubSpot, Salesforce) that may provide:

  • Company firmographic data (industry, size, location)
  • Publicly available business contact information

We do not purchase personal email lists or engage in data brokerage.

3.3.3 Business Partners

If you are referred by a partner or reseller, we may receive your contact information and project details from them with your consent.

4. How We Use Information

We use Personal Information for the following purposes:

4.1 Purposes of Processing

4.1.1 Provide and Operate the Services

  • Create and manage user accounts
  • Authenticate users and manage access controls
  • Deliver Services features (Governor, Policy Engine, Orchestrator)
  • Process API requests and execute workflows
  • Generate audit logs and compliance reports
  • Provide customer support and troubleshooting

Legal basis (GDPR): Contract performance, legitimate interests

4.1.2 Improve and Develop Services

  • Analyze usage patterns to identify improvement opportunities
  • Develop new features and functionality
  • Conduct research and analytics using aggregated, anonymized data
  • Test new features and conduct A/B testing

Legal basis (GDPR): Legitimate interests

Important: We do not use Customer Data (your policies, workflows, or audit logs) to train AI models or machine learning algorithms. Service improvements are based on anonymized usage metrics, not your proprietary data.

4.1.3 Communicate with You

  • Send transactional emails (account notifications, password resets, service updates)
  • Respond to inquiries and support requests
  • Send marketing communications with your consent or where permitted by law
  • Conduct surveys and request feedback

Legal basis (GDPR): Contract performance, legitimate interests, consent for marketing

4.1.4 Marketing and Business Development

  • Promote Services and features
  • Organize webinars, events, and demonstrations
  • Deliver targeted advertising with consent where required
  • Analyze marketing campaign effectiveness

Legal basis (GDPR): Consent, legitimate interests where consent is not required

4.1.5 Security and Fraud Prevention

  • Monitor for unauthorized access and security threats
  • Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, or policy violations
  • Conduct security audits and incident response
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and Acceptable Use Policy

Legal basis (GDPR): Legitimate interests, legal obligations

4.1.6 Compliance and Legal Obligations

  • Comply with applicable laws and regulations
  • Respond to legal process (subpoenas, court orders)
  • Enforce our legal rights and defend against claims
  • Maintain records for audit and regulatory purposes

Legal basis (GDPR): Legal obligations, legitimate interests

4.2 AI and Machine Learning

We do not train AI models on your Customer Data.

  • Your policies, workflows, decision logs, and proprietary data are never used to train AI systems
  • Service improvements are based on aggregated, anonymized usage metrics (e.g., average policy evaluation time across all customers)
  • If we develop AI features in the future that would involve processing Customer Data differently, we will obtain your explicit consent

4.3 Automated Decision-Making

We do not use Personal Information for automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects, except:

  • Automated fraud detection (which may flag suspicious activity for human review)
  • Access control decisions based on role-based permissions you configure

You have the right to object to automated decisions and request human review.

5. How We Share Information

5.1 We Do Not Sell Personal Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade Personal Information to third parties for monetary or other valuable consideration. This applies to all users, including California residents covered by CCPA.

5.2 Sharing with Service Providers (Processors)

We share Personal Information with trusted third-party service providers who perform functions on our behalf:

5.2.1 Cloud Infrastructure Providers

  • Examples: AWS, Google Cloud, Azure for managed cloud deployments
  • Purpose: Hosting Services, data storage, compute resources
  • Data shared: Account information, usage data, Customer Data (encrypted)

5.2.2 Analytics and Monitoring Tools

  • Examples: Google Analytics, Datadog, Prometheus
  • Purpose: Site/Service analytics, performance monitoring
  • Data shared: Usage data, log files (anonymized where possible)

5.2.3 Communication and Support Tools

  • Examples: SendGrid (email), Intercom (chat support), Zendesk (ticketing)
  • Purpose: Send transactional emails, provide customer support
  • Data shared: Contact information, support conversation history

5.2.4 Marketing and CRM Platforms

  • Examples: HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp
  • Purpose: Marketing campaigns, lead management, customer relationship management
  • Data shared: Contact information, marketing preferences, interaction data

5.2.5 Payment Processors

  • Examples: Stripe, PayPal (if applicable)
  • Purpose: Process payments and subscriptions
  • Data shared: Billing information (we do not store credit card numbers)

All service providers:

  • Are contractually obligated to protect Personal Information
  • May only use information to perform services on our behalf
  • Are listed in our Subprocessor Directory

We provide 30 days notice before adding new subprocessors.

5.3 Sharing with Affiliates

We may share Personal Information with affiliates (companies under common ownership or control) for:

  • Internal administration and reporting
  • Joint marketing or business development with your consent

Affiliates are subject to the same privacy commitments as Axiosky.

5.4 Business Transfers

If Axiosky is involved in a merger, acquisition, asset sale, or bankruptcy, Personal Information may be transferred to the successor entity. We will:

  • Notify you via email and Site notice
  • Ensure the successor entity honors this Privacy Policy or obtain your consent to a new policy

5.5 Legal and Regulatory Disclosures

We may disclose Personal Information when required by law or when we believe disclosure is necessary to:

  • Comply with legal process (subpoenas, court orders, government requests)
  • Enforce our Terms of Service or Acceptable Use Policy
  • Protect our rights, property, or safety, or that of our users or the public
  • Prevent fraud, security threats, or illegal activity

We will:

  • Challenge overbroad or unlawful requests where feasible
  • Notify you of legal requests unless prohibited by law or court order
  • Disclose only the minimum information necessary to comply

5.6 With Your Consent

We may share Personal Information for purposes not described in this Privacy Policy with your explicit consent.

5.7 Aggregated and Anonymized Data

We may share aggregated or anonymized data that cannot reasonably identify individuals, such as:

  • Industry benchmarks and usage statistics
  • Research findings and whitepapers
  • Marketing materials and case studies

This data is not considered Personal Information.

6. Data Security

Axiosky implements robust technical, organizational, and administrative safeguards to protect your data:

6.1 Security Measures

  • End-to-end encryption for data in transit (TLS 1.3+)
  • Encryption at rest using industry-standard algorithms (AES-256)
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for account protection
  • Regular security audits, penetration testing, and vulnerability assessments
  • Intrusion detection and prevention systems
  • Access controls and role-based permissions
  • Secure API design and authentication mechanisms

6.2 Compliance Standards

  • SOC 2 Type II: Audited controls for security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy
  • GDPR: Compliant data processing for EU/EEA residents
  • CCPA/CPRA: Compliant with California privacy law
  • HIPAA: Ready for healthcare deployments with BAA
  • FedRAMP: Pursuing authorization for government deployments

6.3 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach affecting Personal Information, we will:

  • Notify affected individuals without undue delay (typically within 72 hours of discovery)
  • Notify applicable supervisory authorities as required by law
  • Provide information about the breach, affected data, and remediation steps

7. Data Retention

7.1 Retention Periods

We retain Personal Information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law.

Data Category Retention Period Rationale
Account information Duration of account + 30 days Provide Services, comply with legal obligations
Usage and log data 90 days Security monitoring, troubleshooting
Audit logs 7 years (default, configurable) Regulatory compliance, customer requirements
Marketing data Until opt-out + 30 days Marketing communications, consent management
Billing records 7 years Tax and accounting compliance
Support tickets 3 years Customer support, service improvement

7.2 Deletion Upon Request

You may request deletion of your Personal Information. After verification, we will delete information within 30 days, except where retention is required by:

  • Legal or regulatory obligations (e.g., tax records, audit logs)
  • Ongoing litigation or investigations
  • Legitimate business purposes (e.g., fraud prevention, security)

7.3 Customer Data Retention

Customer Data (policies, workflows, audit logs) retention is governed by your configuration and our Terms of Service:

  • Active subscriptions: Retained as long as Services are in use
  • After termination: 30-day grace period for data export, then deletion (except immutable audit logs required for compliance)

Immutable audit logs: By design, audit logs cannot be deleted (compliance feature). If you require audit log deletion for privacy reasons, we can unlink pseudonymous identifiers.

8. Your Privacy Rights

8.1 Rights for All Users

8.1.1 Access and Portability

  • Right to access: Request a copy of the Personal Information we hold about you
  • Right to data portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (e.g., JSON, CSV)

8.1.3 Deletion

  • Right to erasure: Request deletion of your Personal Information (subject to legal retention requirements)

8.1.5 Withdraw Consent

  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time (does not affect lawfulness of prior processing)

8.2 Additional Rights for GDPR (EEA, UK, Switzerland)

If you are in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you have additional rights under GDPR:

  • Right to access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to rectification: Correct or update inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations)
  • Right to restrict processing: Limit how we process your data
  • Right to data portability: Receive your data in a structured, portable format
  • Right to object: Opt out of marketing communications and certain processing
  • Right to withdraw consent: Revoke consent for specific processing activities

Supervisory Authority: You have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority (see EDPB member list).

8.3 California Resident Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

Categories of Personal Information collected (last 12 months):

  • Identifiers (name, email, IP address)
  • Professional information (company, job title)
  • Internet activity (usage data, logs)
  • Inferences (preferences derived from usage)

Business purposes for collection:

  • Providing Services and customer support
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Service improvement and analytics
  • Marketing communications with consent

Categories of third parties with whom we share information:

  • Service providers (cloud hosts, analytics, support tools)
  • Affiliates for internal administration
  • Legal/regulatory authorities when required by law

We do not sell Personal Information.

Your California Privacy Rights:

  • Right to know, delete, and opt-out (see above)
  • Contact: privacy@axiosky.com

8.4 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@axiosky.com with your request. We will respond within the legally required timeframe (typically 30 days for GDPR, 45 days for CCPA). We may request proof of identity to verify your request.
