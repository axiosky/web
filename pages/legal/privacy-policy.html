Introduction

Axiosky ("we," "us," "our," or the "Company") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, use our services, and interact with our platform.

This policy applies to all information collected through our website, mobile applications, and services (collectively, the "Services"), as well as offline when you communicate with us.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our Services. By accessing and using Axiosky, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by all the provisions of this Privacy Policy.

What Data We Collect

We collect information in various ways, including information you provide directly and information collected automatically as you interact with our Services.

Information You Provide

  • Account Information: First and last name, email address, phone number, company name, job title, password, and any other information you provide during registration or profile creation.
  • Communication Data: Messages, feedback, inquiries, and any other communications you send us through contact forms, email, or chat.
  • Payment Information: Billing address, payment method details (processed securely by third-party payment processorsâ€”we do not store full card numbers).
  • Pilot & Engagement Data: Project descriptions, compliance requirements, risk assessments, and other information shared during pilot requests or sales discussions.
  • Documents & Uploads: Any files, documents, or data you upload to our platform for processing or governance analysis.

Automatically Collected Information

  • Log Data: IP address, browser type, operating system, referring/exit pages, device identifiers, and timestamps of your access to our Services.
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, features accessed, time spent on pages, clicks, scroll depth, and interaction patterns.
  • Device Information: Device type, operating system version, unique device identifiers, mobile network information.
  • Cookies & Similar Technologies: We use cookies, web beacons, pixels, and similar technologies to remember preferences, measure performance, and enable analytics.
  • Location Data: General geolocation inferred from IP address (not precise geolocation unless you provide it).

Third-Party Data

  • Information from public sources, industry databases, or data enrichment providers to enhance our understanding of business contexts.
  • Information from partners, integrations, or single sign-on providers (e.g., OAuth with GitHub, Google, or Microsoft).

How We Use Your Data

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

Service Delivery & Operations

  • Providing, operating, and maintaining the Services.
  • Creating and managing your account and profile.
  • Processing transactions and sending transaction confirmations.
  • Delivering customer support and responding to inquiries.
  • Hosting pilot programs and bounded engagements.

Personalization & Improvement

  • Personalizing your experience and remembering your preferences.
  • Analyzing usage patterns to improve platform features and functionality.
  • Conducting research, analytics, and product development.
  • A/B testing and feature experimentation.

Marketing & Communication

  • Sending promotional emails, newsletters, and updates (with your consent where required).
  • Notifying you of changes to our Services or policies.
  • Responding to your inquiries and feedback.
  • Conducting surveys and gathering customer feedback.

Compliance & Security

  • Enforcing our Terms of Service and other legal agreements.
  • Preventing fraud, abuse, and illegal activity.
  • Protecting the security and integrity of our Services.
  • Complying with legal obligations and regulatory requirements (GDPR, CCPA, SOC 2, etc.).
  • Responding to government requests, court orders, and lawful investigations.

Legitimate Business Interests

  • Understanding market trends and competitive landscape.
  • Optimizing our operations and business efficiency.
  • Building partnerships and exploring strategic opportunities.

Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. However, we may share your data in limited circumstances:

Service Providers

We engage third-party vendors and service providers to assist with:

  • Cloud hosting and infrastructure (AWS, Google Cloud, etc.)
  • Payment processing (Stripe, PayPal, etc.)
  • Email delivery and CRM platforms
  • Analytics providers (Mixpanel, Segment, etc.)
  • Customer support tools and helpdesk software
  • Security monitoring and fraud detection

These vendors are contractually obligated to use your data only for the services they provide and must maintain appropriate security safeguards.

Legal & Regulatory Obligations

We may disclose your information if required by law, court order, regulatory authority, or government request. We will notify you of such requests unless prohibited by law.

Business Transactions

If Axiosky is involved in a merger, acquisition, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change and any choices you may have regarding your information.

Pilot Participants & Collaborators

During pilot programs, we may share limited information with your designated team members and authorized collaborators to facilitate the engagement. All participants sign NDAs.

Aggregated & Anonymized Data

We may share aggregated, de-identified statistics, industry benchmarks, and anonymized insights with partners, research institutions, and publicly without restriction.

Data Security & Compliance

Axiosky implements robust technical, organizational, and administrative safeguards to protect your data:

Security Measures

  • End-to-end encryption for data in transit (TLS 1.3+).
  • Encryption at rest using industry-standard algorithms (AES-256).
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for account protection.
  • Regular security audits, penetration testing, and vulnerability assessments.
  • Intrusion detection and prevention systems.
  • Access controls and role-based permissions.
  • Secure API design and authentication mechanisms.

Compliance Standards

  • SOC 2 Type II: Audited controls for security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.
  • GDPR: Compliant data processing for EU/EEA residents.
  • CCPA/CPRA: Compliant with California privacy law.
  • HIPAA: Ready for healthcare deployments with BAA.
  • FedRAMP: Pursuing authorization for government deployments.

Limitations

While we employ comprehensive security measures, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your data. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your password and account access.

Your Rights & Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights:

GDPR Rights (EU/EEA Residents)

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data.
  • Right to Rectification: Correct or update inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations).
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we process your data.
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, portable format.
  • Right to Object: Opt out of marketing communications and certain processing.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Revoke consent for specific processing activities.

CCPA/CPRA Rights (California Residents)

  • Right to Know: Request categories and sources of data collected.
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of collected data.
  • Right to Opt-Out: Opt out of data sales or sharing (we do not sell data).
  • Right to Correct: Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate for exercising your rights.

General Rights

  • Opt out of marketing emails by clicking the unsubscribe link.
  • Disable cookies through your browser settings (though this may impact functionality).
  • Request a manual review of automated decisions.
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@axiosky.com with your request. We will respond within the legally required timeframe (typically 30 days for GDPR, 45 days for CCPA). We may request proof of identity to verify your request.

Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as necessary to provide the Services and fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy:

  • Account Information: Retained while you maintain an active account, plus 30 days after account deletion or termination.
  • Transaction Records: Retained for 7 years to comply with tax and financial regulations.
  • Pilot & Project Data: Retained for the duration of the engagement plus 2 years for audit and compliance purposes.
  • Marketing Communications: Retained until you unsubscribe; contact data retained for 3 years post-unsubscribe.
  • Log & Analytics Data: Retained for 90 days; aggregated analytics retained indefinitely.
  • Security Incidents: Retained for 3 years post-incident for forensic and compliance review.

You may request earlier deletion of your data at any time, subject to legal retention obligations.
