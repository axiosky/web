1. Introduction and Scope

1.1 Overview

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") establishes guidelines and restrictions for the use of Axiosky's governance control plane services, including the Governor, Policy Engine, Orchestrator, and related offerings (collectively, the "Services").

The AUP applies to all customers, users, and authorized representatives who access or use the Services. By accessing the Services, you agree to comply with this AUP and all applicable laws and regulations.

1.2 Relationship to Other Agreements

This AUP is incorporated into and forms part of your agreement with Axiosky, including the Terms of Service and any executed Service Agreement or Data Processing Agreement. In the event of a conflict between this AUP and other terms, the terms providing greater restrictions on use shall apply.

1.3 Purpose

The purpose of this AUP is to:

  • Protect the security, integrity, and availability of the Services for all users
  • Ensure responsible and ethical use of AI governance capabilities
  • Prevent unauthorized access, data breaches, and malicious activity
  • Maintain compliance with applicable laws and regulations
  • Protect intellectual property and confidential information
  • Promote fair resource allocation and service reliability

2. Authorized Use

2.1 General Authorization

You may use the Services solely for lawful purposes and in accordance with this AUP. Authorized uses include:

  • Deploying and managing governance policies and workflows within your organization
  • Orchestrating autonomous agents with oversight and accountability controls
  • Monitoring decision-making processes and compliance requirements
  • Generating audit logs and governance reports
  • Configuring integrations with authorized third-party systems
  • Accessing documentation and support resources
  • Conducting security testing and vulnerability assessments with prior written authorization

2.2 Scope of Use Rights

Your use of the Services is restricted to:

  • Your organization and authorized users with valid credentials
  • The specific deployment model and capacity limits outlined in your Service Agreement
  • The geographic jurisdictions permitted under applicable export control regulations
  • Compliance with the terms and conditions of your subscription

2.3 Account Responsibility

You are responsible for:

  • Maintaining the confidentiality of your account credentials, API keys, and access tokens
  • Monitoring account activity and reporting unauthorized access immediately
  • Ensuring all authorized users understand and comply with this AUP
  • Notifying Axiosky of changes to your organization or authorized users
  • Disabling or rotating credentials for terminated employees or contractors promptly

3. Prohibited Activities

3.1 Illegal Activities

You may not use the Services for any illegal purpose or in violation of any applicable law or regulation, including but not limited to:

  • Activities that violate export control laws, sanctions, or embargoes
  • Activities that facilitate money laundering, fraud, or financial crimes
  • Activities that support terrorism, human trafficking, or other criminal enterprises
  • Activities that infringe intellectual property rights or trade secrets
  • Activities that violate data protection or privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

3.2 Unauthorized Access and System Compromise

You may not:

  • Access or attempt to access accounts, systems, or data without authorization
  • Engage in unauthorized penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, or security assessments without written permission
  • Attempt to bypass, circumvent, or disable security controls or authentication mechanisms
  • Share, sell, or distribute access credentials or API keys to unauthorized parties
  • Exploit security vulnerabilities or zero-day exploits
  • Reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Services or related software

3.3 Malicious Activity

You may not use the Services to:

  • Transmit malware, viruses, worms, ransomware, or other malicious code
  • Deploy botnets or coordinate distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks
  • Conduct phishing, social engineering, or credential harvesting attacks
  • Introduce trojan horses or backdoors into the infrastructure
  • Engage in spamming, unsolicited communications, or abusive behavior

3.4 Data Violations and Misuse

You may not:

  • Upload, store, or process data in violation of applicable privacy laws
  • Transmit personally identifiable information (PII) without proper encryption or safeguards
  • Sell, trade, or share customer or user data for purposes unrelated to providing services
  • Extract or exfiltrate data from the Services for unauthorized purposes
  • Use the Services to conduct surveillance or unauthorized monitoring of individuals
  • Process sensitive data (health, biometric, financial information) without explicit authorization and safeguards

3.5 Resource Abuse and Performance Degradation

You may not:

  • Generate artificial or excessive load on the Services beyond your subscription limits
  • Utilize the Services as a proxy, VPN, or anonymization tool
  • Perform activities that significantly degrade service performance for other users
  • Deploy cryptocurrency mining or computationally intensive activities unrelated to governance
  • Conduct automated scraping, crawling, or data extraction at excessive rates
  • Make API requests that exceed rate limits or fair use thresholds

3.6 Harmful and Offensive Content

You may not upload, store, or transmit content that is:

  • Defamatory, libelous, or otherwise false and harmful
  • Obscene, sexually explicit, or indecent
  • Hateful, discriminatory, or promoting violence against individuals or groups
  • Harassing, bullying, or intimidating
  • Invasive of privacy or violating a person's reasonable expectation of privacy

3.7 Competitive and Commercial Misuse

You may not:

  • Use the Services for competitive analysis or benchmarking of Axiosky's capabilities for a competitor
  • Republish or redistribute the Services or their features as a standalone product
  • Use the Services to develop competing governance platforms or functionality
  • Make false claims about the Services' capabilities, performance, or compliance status

4. Customer Data Responsibilities

4.1 Data Classification and Handling

You are responsible for:

  • Classifying and labeling data according to sensitivity levels (public, internal, confidential, restricted)
  • Implementing appropriate access controls and encryption for sensitive data
  • Obtaining necessary consents and approvals before processing personal data
  • Ensuring data quality and accuracy in policies, configurations, and workflows
  • Maintaining accurate records of data processing activities

4.2 Prohibited Data Categories

Without explicit written authorization, you may not store or process in the Services:

  • Unencrypted payment card information (PCI-DSS restricted)
  • Biometric data or genetic information without appropriate safeguards
  • Unauthorized health or medical records (unless HIPAA-compliant deployment)
  • Trade secrets or proprietary information of third parties without authorization
  • Large datasets of personal data from individuals without explicit consent

4.3 Data Retention and Deletion

You must:

  • Specify retention periods for your data based on business and legal requirements
  • Request deletion or archival of data that is no longer needed
  • Comply with data subject requests for deletion or portability
  • Understand that audit logs are immutable for compliance reasons and cannot be deleted

4.4 Audit Log Integrity

Audit logs generated by the Governor are immutable and tamper-proof by design. You may not:

  • Attempt to modify, delete, or tamper with audit logs
  • Use the Services to cover up unauthorized access or policy violations
  • Deliberately create false or misleading governance records

5. Security and Compliance

5.1 Security Incident Reporting

You agree to:

  • Report suspected security vulnerabilities or breaches to security@axiosky.com immediately
  • Not publicly disclose vulnerabilities before Axiosky has had an opportunity to remediate (responsible disclosure)
  • Cooperate with security incident investigations and remediation efforts
  • Implement recommended security patches and updates promptly

5.2 Regulatory Compliance

You are responsible for ensuring your use of the Services complies with:

  • Applicable data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA, LGPD, etc.)
  • Industry-specific regulations (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, FedRAMP, etc.)
  • Export control regulations (EAR, ITAR, sanctions programs)
  • AI governance and algorithmic accountability requirements (EU AI Act, etc.)
  • Contractual obligations and agreements with data subjects and third parties

5.3 Audit and Compliance Verification

You permit Axiosky to:

  • Audit your use of the Services for compliance with this AUP and applicable laws
  • Monitor aggregated and anonymized usage metrics for compliance and improvement purposes
  • Request access to audit logs and compliance documentation upon reasonable notice
  • Conduct security assessments or penetration testing with your consent

5.4 Misalignment with Governance Principles

The Services are designed to enforce accountability and responsible AI governance. Using the Services to circumvent intended governance controls or accountability measures may violate this AUP, including:

  • Disabling audit logging or governance oversight mechanisms
  • Creating policies that bypass required approvals or decision-making processes
  • Using policies to discriminate or make biased decisions
  • Obstructing legitimate governance inquiries or audits

6. Resource Usage and Performance

6.1 Fair Usage Thresholds

You agree to use the Services in a manner consistent with fair usage principles:

  • Policy Evaluations: Normal governance decision-making volume appropriate to your organization size
  • API Requests: Requests within documented rate limits and your subscription tier
  • Storage: Data storage commensurate with your subscription allocation
  • Concurrent Users: Usage within the number of concurrent user seats purchased

6.2 Resource Optimization

You commit to:

  • Implementing efficient policies and workflows to minimize computational overhead
  • Archiving or removing policies that are no longer in use
  • Consolidating redundant decision-making logic
  • Working with Axiosky support to optimize resource consumption

6.3 Excessive Use and Throttling

If your usage significantly exceeds fair usage thresholds, Axiosky reserves the right to:

  • Notify you of excessive usage and request optimization
  • Implement rate limiting or throttling to protect service stability
  • Require upgrading to a higher tier subscription
  • Suspend service access if excessive usage continues after notice

7. Intellectual Property and Content

7.1 Axiosky Intellectual Property

The Services, including all software, documentation, designs, and functionality, are the exclusive property of Axiosky and protected by copyright, trademark, and other intellectual property laws. You agree not to:

  • Copy, modify, or create derivative works from the Services
  • Reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Services
  • Remove or obscure copyright, trademark, or proprietary notices
  • Use the Services to develop competitive offerings

7.2 Your Content Rights

You retain all rights to content you upload to the Services, including policies, workflows, configurations, and data. By uploading content, you:

  • Grant Axiosky a limited license to process and store your content for service delivery
  • Represent that you own or have the right to use such content
  • Warrant that your content does not infringe any third-party rights

7.3 Third-Party Content and Attribution

If your policies or workflows reference or incorporate third-party content, you must:

  • Obtain necessary licenses or permissions
  • Provide appropriate attribution and credit
  • Comply with the terms and conditions of the third-party content

8. AI Governance and Decision-Making

8.1 Responsible AI Use

The Services are designed to enforce governance and accountability for autonomous agents and AI systems. You commit to using the Services to:

  • Implement oversight and approval requirements for critical decisions
  • Maintain transparency in decision-making processes
  • Ensure human review and intervention capabilities
  • Audit and log all autonomous decisions

8.2 Prohibited AI Governance Misuse

You may not use the Services to:

  • Deploy AI systems for autonomous decision-making without human oversight for decisions with legal or material effects
  • Create policies that discriminate based on protected characteristics (race, gender, age, disability, etc.)
  • Circumvent required approval workflows to enable unaccountable autonomous decision-making
  • Deliberately introduce bias or unfair decision rules
  • Use AI governance as cover for human discrimination or unethical practices

8.3 Bias and Fairness

You are responsible for:

  • Testing and validating policies for bias and fairness before deployment
  • Monitoring decisions for unintended bias or adverse impact
  • Documenting assumptions and limitations in your governance policies
  • Implementing human review processes for sensitive or high-impact decisions

8.4 Transparency and Explainability

You must maintain:

  • Clear documentation of policy intent, logic, and decision criteria
  • Audit trails that explain how and why decisions were made
  • Mechanisms for individuals to request explanations for decisions affecting them
  • Records of policy reviews and modifications

9. Third-Party Integrations

9.1 Authorized Integrations

You may integrate the Services with authorized third-party platforms (cloud providers, identity providers, monitoring tools, etc.). For all integrations, you must:

  • Ensure the integration is authorized and complies with the third party's terms of service
  • Obtain necessary API keys or credentials through official channels
  • Not share credentials between users or organizations
  • Comply with the third party's data handling and security requirements

9.2 Data Sharing with Third Parties

When integrating with third parties, you are responsible for:

  • Understanding what data will be shared and for what purposes
  • Obtaining necessary consents from data subjects (where applicable)
  • Complying with data transfer requirements and agreements
  • Reviewing the third party's privacy policy and security practices

9.3 Unsupported Integrations

Axiosky is not responsible for:

  • Data loss or exposure through unsupported or unauthorized integrations
  • Compliance violations resulting from third-party service use
  • Security incidents or vulnerabilities in third-party integrations

10. Enforcement and Remedies

10.1 Monitoring and Detection

Axiosky monitors usage patterns to detect violations of this AUP. Detection methods include:

  • Automated analysis of usage metrics and patterns
  • Security scanning and threat detection
  • Review of audit logs and access records
  • User and third-party reports of suspected violations

10.2 Response to Violations

Upon detection of a violation, Axiosky may take any of the following actions, in its sole discretion:

  • Warning: Notify you of the violation and request immediate remediation
  • Suspension: Temporarily suspend access to the Services until the violation is remedied
  • Termination: Permanently terminate your access to the Services and account
  • Content Removal: Remove or quarantine violating content
  • Legal Action: Pursue legal remedies including damages and injunctive relief

10.3 Severity and Context

The severity of response depends on factors including:

  • The nature and severity of the violation
  • Whether the violation is intentional or unintentional
  • Your history of compliance or prior violations
  • Impact to other users or the Services
  • Cooperation in remediation efforts

10.4 Appeal Process

If your account is suspended or terminated for AUP violations, you may:

  • Submit a written appeal to legal@axiosky.com within 30 days
  • Present evidence that the violation was non-intentional or has been remedied
  • Receive a decision within 15 business days

10.5 Statutory Violations

If you violate laws or regulations (especially export controls, sanctions, or data protection laws), Axiosky may:

  • Immediately suspend or terminate your access
  • Report the violation to relevant authorities
  • Cooperate with law enforcement investigations
  • Not provide advance notice if required by law

11. Contact and Reporting

11.1 AUP Violations and Concerns

To report a suspected AUP violation or raise concerns:

  • General Inquiries: compliance@axiosky.com
  • Security Issues: security@axiosky.com
  • Legal Matters: legal@axiosky.com

11.2 Policy Updates

Axiosky may update this AUP from time to time. We will notify you of material changes via email or in-product notice. Your continued use of the Services constitutes acceptance of the updated AUP.

11.3 Clarification and Questions

If you have questions about what is or is not permitted under this AUP, contact Axiosky support before proceeding with an activity. Axiosky is committed to providing clear guidance and helping you use the Services responsibly.

By using the Services, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Acceptable Use Policy.
