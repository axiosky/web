1. Introduction and Scope

1.1 Parties and Definitions

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between:

Data Controller ("Customer," "you," or "your"):
The entity that has agreed to the Axiosky Terms of Service and uses the Services to process Personal Data.

Data Processor ("Axiosky," "we," "us," or "our"):
Axiosky Inc., a Delaware corporation, providing governance control plane services.

1.2 Key Definitions

  • "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, as defined under applicable Data Protection Laws.
  • "Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, deletion, or any other handling.
  • "Data Protection Laws" means applicable data protection and privacy laws, including GDPR (EU) 2016/679, UK Data Protection Act 2018, UK GDPR, Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (FADP), CCPA and CPRA for California residents, and other applicable privacy laws.
  • "Services" means Axiosky's governance control plane, including Governor, Policy Engine, Orchestrator, and related services.
  • "Customer Data" means data uploaded, processed, or transmitted by Customer through the Services, which may include Personal Data.
  • "Sub-processor" means any third-party processor engaged by Axiosky to process Personal Data on Customer's behalf.
  • "Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed.
  • "Supervisory Authority" means a data protection authority with jurisdiction over Customer's processing activities.

1.3 Scope and Applicability

This DPA applies to:

  • Processing of Personal Data by Axiosky on Customer's behalf in connection with the Services
  • Customer Data that includes Personal Data of individuals in jurisdictions covered by Data Protection Laws

Important: This DPA does not apply to Personal Data we collect as a data controller (e.g., Customer's account information, which is governed by our Privacy Policy).

1.4 Roles and Responsibilities

Customer is the Data Controller, meaning Customer:

  • Determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data
  • Is responsible for compliance with Data Protection Laws
  • Provides lawful instructions to Axiosky regarding processing
  • Ensures it has a lawful basis for processing Personal Data

Axiosky is the Data Processor, meaning Axiosky:

  • Processes Personal Data only on Customer's documented instructions
  • Implements appropriate technical and organizational measures
  • Assists Customer in meeting Data Protection Law obligations
  • Does not process Personal Data for its own purposes (except as required by law or to provide Services)

2. Data Processing Details

2.1 Subject Matter and Nature of Processing

Subject Matter: Processing of Personal Data contained in Customer Data to provide the Services as described in the Terms of Service.

Nature and Purpose of Processing:

  • Hosting and storing Customer Data (including Personal Data)
  • Executing governance workflows and policy evaluations
  • Coordinating multi-agent operations
  • Generating audit logs and compliance reports
  • Providing customer support and troubleshooting
  • Ensuring platform security and reliability

2.2 Duration of Processing

Processing begins when Customer first uploads or transmits Personal Data to the Services and continues until:

  • Termination or expiration of the Terms of Service
  • Deletion of Personal Data as requested by Customer
  • End of the data retention period (default: 30 days post-termination for operational data; 7 years for audit logs)

2.3 Categories of Data Subjects

Personal Data processed may relate to:

  • Customer's employees, contractors, and agents
  • End users or customers of Customer's systems
  • Individuals whose data is governed by policies deployed through the Services
  • Other individuals whose Personal Data Customer chooses to process

Customer determines the specific categories based on its use case.

2.4 Types of Personal Data

Personal Data processed may include (depending on Customer's use case):

  • Identifiers: Names, email addresses, user IDs, IP addresses, device identifiers, employee identification numbers
  • Professional Data: Job titles, roles, department affiliations, company names, organizational hierarchies
  • Usage and Activity Data: Actions within workflows, policy evaluation records, timestamps, session data
  • Decision Records: Governance decisions, approvals, rejections, audit trails

2.5 Frequency and Volume

The frequency and volume of Personal Data processing depends on:

  • Customer's deployment size and user base
  • Number of policies and workflows executed
  • Complexity of governance rules and agent orchestration
  • Audit logging and compliance reporting needs

Customer is responsible for ensuring its Personal Data volumes align with its subscription tier.

3. Processor Obligations

3.1 Processing Instructions

Axiosky processes Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer, including with respect to:

  • The subject matter and duration of processing
  • The nature and purpose of processing
  • Types of Personal Data and categories of Data Subjects
  • Objectives of processing (as specified in Customer's policies)

Customer must provide clear, unambiguous written instructions. Any changes to processing instructions require a documented amendment to this DPA or written notice from Customer.

3.2 Confidentiality

Axiosky ensures that:

  • Persons authorized to process Personal Data are subject to confidentiality obligations
  • Confidentiality commitments survive termination of their employment or engagement
  • Personnel are trained on Data Protection Law compliance and security practices
  • Access to Personal Data is restricted to personnel with a legitimate need to know

3.3 Sub-processor Management

Axiosky:

  • May engage Sub-processors to process Personal Data (see Section 4)
  • Maintains a current list of authorized Sub-processors available upon request
  • Provides notice of changes to Sub-processors (minimum 30 days advance notice)
  • Remains liable to Customer for Sub-processor performance

3.4 Data Subject Rights Support

Axiosky assists Customer in fulfilling Data Subject rights by:

  • Providing Customer with access to Personal Data upon request
  • Facilitating correction or deletion of Personal Data
  • Supporting data portability requests
  • Assisting with Data Subject consent withdrawal
  • Cooperating with Data Subject requests for information about processing

Customer remains responsible for responding to Data Subjects within legally required timeframes.

3.5 Data Protection Impact Assessment

Axiosky provides Customer with necessary information to conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) and supports Customer's compliance obligations. Upon request, Axiosky will:

  • Provide documentation of its processing activities
  • Describe security measures and safeguards
  • Support Customer's assessment of privacy risks
  • Cooperate with regulatory authority inquiries

4. Sub-Processors

4.1 Authorized Sub-processors

Axiosky engages the following categories of Sub-processors to provide the Services:

  • Cloud Infrastructure Providers: AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure (for compute, storage, and networking)
  • Analytics and Monitoring: Datadog, Prometheus, ELK Stack (for service monitoring and performance)
  • Identity and Access Management: Okta, Auth0 (for authentication and authorization)
  • Communication Services: SendGrid, Intercom (for transactional communications and support)
  • Payment Processing: Stripe, PayPal (for subscription billing)

4.2 Sub-processor Agreements

Axiosky ensures all Sub-processors are bound by data processing agreements that provide:

  • Equivalent data protection commitments to this DPA
  • Restrictions on sub-sub-processing without authorization
  • Audit rights for Axiosky and Customer
  • Liability for data breaches or non-compliance

4.3 Notification of Changes

Axiosky will notify Customer of:

  • New Sub-processors (minimum 30 days advance notice)
  • Changes to existing Sub-processor responsibilities
  • Removal or replacement of Sub-processors

Customer may object to new Sub-processors on reasonable grounds. If Customer objects, Customer may terminate the affected Services without penalty.

4.4 Sub-processor Directory

A current list of Sub-processors is maintained at https://axiosky.com/legal/sub-processors and is updated regularly.

5. Data Subject Rights

5.1 Customer's Obligations

Customer remains responsible for responding to Data Subject requests within required timeframes. These rights include:

  • Right of Access: Data Subjects may request a copy of their Personal Data
  • Right to Rectification: Data Subjects may request correction of inaccurate Personal Data
  • Right to Erasure: Data Subjects may request deletion of their Personal Data (subject to legal retention)
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Data Subjects may limit how we process their data
  • Right to Data Portability: Data Subjects may receive data in a structured, portable format
  • Right to Object: Data Subjects may object to certain processing
  • Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making: Data Subjects may request human review of automated decisions

5.2 Axiosky's Assistance

Axiosky assists Customer by:

  • Providing Customer with access to Personal Data within 5 business days upon request
  • Facilitating correction or deletion within 10 business days
  • Supporting data portability in structured formats (JSON, CSV) within 10 business days
  • Maintaining audit logs showing Data Subject interactions and decisions
  • Cooperating with Customer's responses to regulatory authority inquiries

5.3 Data Subject Inquiries

Data Subjects should direct requests for their Personal Data to Customer, as Customer is the Data Controller. Customer will be responsible for:

  • Verifying Data Subject identity
  • Determining whether the request is valid and compliant with law
  • Extracting Personal Data from the Services
  • Responding to the Data Subject within required timeframes

Axiosky may assist Customer upon request at no additional cost.

6. International Data Transfers

6.1 Data Transfer Mechanisms

To the extent Personal Data is transferred from the European Economic Area, UK, or Switzerland to the United States or other countries without adequacy decisions, Axiosky uses:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): For transfers to the United States and other jurisdictions without adequacy decisions
  • Supplementary Measures: Where appropriate, additional safeguards are implemented
  • Binding Corporate Rules: Where applicable for Axiosky affiliates

6.2 Standard Contractual Clauses

This DPA incorporates Standard Contractual Clauses for data transfers as approved by the European Commission. The SCCs are available upon request from privacy@axiosky.com.

6.3 Transfer Impact Assessment

Axiosky has assessed data transfer mechanisms and maintains appropriate safeguards. Customer may request a copy of our Transfer Impact Assessment documentation.

6.4 Cooperation on Transfer Issues

If laws or regulatory action require suspension of data transfers, Axiosky will:

  • Notify Customer immediately
  • Work with Customer to identify alternative solutions
  • Offer options for data storage in the relevant jurisdiction
  • Ensure compliance with applicable law

7. Security Measures

7.1 Technical and Organizational Measures

Axiosky implements appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, including:

  • Encryption: End-to-end encryption in transit (TLS 1.3+) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control, multi-factor authentication, principle of least privilege
  • Data Isolation: Logical and physical isolation between customer environments
  • Intrusion Detection: Real-time monitoring and threat detection systems
  • Vulnerability Management: Regular security assessments, penetration testing, patch management
  • Audit Logging: Immutable logs of all access and processing activities
  • Incident Response: 24/7 monitoring with documented incident response procedures

7.2 Compliance Certifications

Axiosky maintains the following security certifications and compliance standards:

  • SOC 2 Type II: Audited controls for security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality
  • GDPR: Full compliance with data protection requirements
  • ISO 27001: Information security management system certification (where applicable)
  • HIPAA: Available for healthcare deployments with Business Associate Agreement

7.3 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a confirmed data breach affecting Personal Data:

  • Axiosky will notify Customer without undue delay (typically within 24-72 hours of discovery)
  • Notification will include details of the breach, affected data, and remediation steps
  • Axiosky will cooperate with Customer's regulatory notifications and investigations
  • Customer remains responsible for notifying affected Data Subjects if required by law

8. Audit and Compliance

8.1 Audit Rights

Customer has the right to:

  • Request evidence of Axiosky's compliance with this DPA and Data Protection Laws
  • Audit Axiosky's processing activities upon reasonable notice (typically quarterly with at least 15 days' notice)
  • Conduct audits at Customer's expense; one complimentary audit per year included
  • Engage third-party auditors on Axiosky's premises subject to confidentiality agreements

8.2 Compliance Certification

Axiosky will:

  • Provide copies of SOC 2 Type II reports upon request (under NDA)
  • Certify in writing its compliance with this DPA annually
  • Disclose any material security incidents or compliance violations
  • Support Customer's compliance audits and regulatory inquiries

8.3 Regulatory Authority Cooperation

Axiosky will:

  • Cooperate with Supervisory Authority inquiries regarding Personal Data processing
  • Provide requested information and documentation
  • Notify Customer of any data protection authority investigations or orders
  • Ensure Customer is informed of legal requests for Personal Data

8.4 Sub-processor Audits

Axiosky remains responsible for Sub-processor compliance and will audit Sub-processors as needed to ensure adherence to this DPA.

9. Deletion and Return of Data

9.1 Data Return or Deletion

Upon termination or expiration of the Services, Axiosky will:

  • Delete Customer Data (including Personal Data) within 30 days, except as required by law
  • Certify deletion in writing within 60 days of termination
  • Provide Customer with data export options if requested (within reasonable timeframe before deletion)

9.2 Exception for Immutable Audit Logs

Audit logs generated by the Governor are immutable by design and serve critical compliance purposes. However:

  • Personal Data within audit logs (names, IDs) can be pseudonymized or unlinked at Customer's request
  • Audit log retention follows Customer's configuration (default 7 years)
  • Upon account termination, audit logs are retained only if required by law or regulatory requirement

9.3 Legal Holds

Notwithstanding the above, Axiosky may retain Personal Data if:

  • Required by applicable law or court order
  • Necessary for legal proceedings, investigations, or disputes
  • Necessary to defend against claims or liabilities

Customer will be notified of any legal holds affecting Personal Data.

9.4 Data Destruction Certificate

Upon request, Axiosky will provide a data destruction certificate certifying that all Personal Data has been securely deleted, with details of destruction methods and dates.

10. Liability and Indemnification

10.1 Processor Liability

Axiosky's liability for violations of this DPA is limited as follows:

  • Axiosky is liable for damages caused by processing in violation of this DPA only to the extent caused by Axiosky's failure to comply
  • Axiosky is not liable if Customer caused the violation through its instructions, misuse of the Services, or failure to comply with obligations
  • Liability is capped at the amounts specified in the Terms of Service

10.2 Shared Liability

Liability for certain violations may be shared between Customer and Axiosky if both parties contributed to the violation. In such cases, liability will be apportioned based on comparative fault.

10.3 Indemnification

Axiosky will indemnify Customer for third-party claims that Axiosky's processing of Personal Data violates Data Protection Laws, provided Customer:

  • Promptly notifies Axiosky of the claim
  • Grants Axiosky sole control of the defense
  • Reasonably cooperates in the defense

10.4 Customer Indemnification

Customer will indemnify Axiosky for third-party claims arising from:

  • Customer's misuse of the Services in violation of Data Protection Laws
  • Customer Data that violates third-party rights or applicable law
  • Customer's instructions that violate Data Protection Laws

11. Term and Termination

11.1 Duration

This DPA commences on the Effective Date and continues for as long as Customer uses the Services and Personal Data is processed, unless earlier terminated.

11.2 Termination

This DPA will terminate upon:

  • Termination of the Terms of Service
  • Customer's request to delete all Personal Data
  • Mutual written consent

11.3 Survival

The following sections survive termination of this DPA:

  • Section 8 (Audit and Compliance)
  • Section 9 (Deletion and Return of Data)
  • Section 10 (Liability and Indemnification)
  • Confidentiality obligations

12. Contact and Notices

12.1 Data Protection Officer

For questions about this DPA, data protection inquiries, or Data Subject requests:

Data Protection Officer: dpo@axiosky.com
Privacy Inquiries: privacy@axiosky.com
Legal Matters: legal@axiosky.com

12.2 Amendment and Updates

Axiosky may update this DPA from time to time to reflect:

  • Changes in Data Protection Laws
  • Changes in processing activities or safeguards
  • New Sub-processors or integrations
  • Operational improvements

Material changes will be communicated to Customer with at least 30 days' notice. Customer may terminate the Services if it does not accept the updated DPA.

12.3 Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, USA, without regard to conflicts of law principles. However, in jurisdictions where Data Protection Laws are mandatory (e.g., GDPR), those laws will apply to data protection obligations.

12.4 Entire Agreement

This DPA, together with the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding data processing and Personal Data protection. In the event of a conflict, this DPA prevails regarding Personal Data processing matters.

By using the Services and processing Personal Data through Axiosky, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Data Processing Agreement and agree to be bound by its terms.
